Discovery & Define

Defining my design principles from area of opportunities

Situation

Since GXS Invest is a new project, there were very little understanding of our target users.

Tasks

To understand our users better, I interviewed 4 investors to identify their task, pain points and needs.


Key painpoints:
  • Fear of losing money 
  • Complicated jargons and experience
  • Unsure how much they should be investing and how investing works

Results

Translate area of opportunities into design principles:
  • Lower barrier of entry -> Make the experience easy 
  • Reduce complexity -> Minimal and useful
  • Providing assurance -> Be predictable

Design

1. GXS Invest home page

User goal

  • Know historical performance of investment fund
  • Perform main actions like buy & sell
  • Keep track of their asset performance
  • View transactions

Painpoints based on competitor's home pages

  • Complex terms
  • Too much information, very cluttered

Explorations

Based on user goals and pain points, I brainstormed 2 concepts of how the home page may look like.

Exploration 1: Single value + graph

To keep it simple, my first exploration focus on displaying 1 value, the average yearly performance, for new investors. For detailed analysis, user can tap to reveal graph

Exploration 2: Multiple values

Taking into account dev effort and that not all users can read a graph, the second exploration left out the graph but instead showed a breakdown of performance based on different time period.

Guerilla testing

To make sure I was on the right track, I did quick testing to gather intial feedback.

Key feedback:
  • Like the idea of only 1 value - think its less confusing
  • Misunderstood value as performance of their owned assets
  • Expect to see their assets on top and will be slightly anxious if its not obvious
  • Knowing how much their investment may grow is easier to understand and bigger motivation than %

Final design

Based on the feedback, I made changes and proposed a new design. The proposed design then went through usability testing resulting in my final design.

Key changes:
  • Moved 'total assets' up
  • Stick to a single value & added context to what it is
  • Removed graph as user mentioned its not a must have for MVP

    2. Managing business needs with design

    Business need

    Business insisted on displaying loan products on the website like items on catalog.

    1 product = 1 detail page & application form

    With many similar loan offerings, applicants cannot differentiate between similar loans. 40% applied wrong loan, increasing TAT by 30mins for each reassignment.

    Solution

    We shared our product research that compares each loan's features to convince business some products features are too similar.

    Then, suggested combining loans with similar purpose on UI level but retain business needs of keeping individual products.

    Result

    Collaborated with tech to design a flow where backend system helps to assign products based on eligibility checks: 0% of applicants applied for wrong products.

    3. Streamlining customer journey

    Problem

    All approved loans required applicants and guarantors to meet relationship managers physically to verify and accept loans.

    Solution

    Applicants & guarantors are now able to:
    • Verify identity with Singpass or manually by uploading IC & NOA
    • Accept loan offer online 

    Result

    • 80% of applicants verified through Singpass
    • Atleast 50% of applicants qualified to accept loans online 

    User validation

    Online user interviews with 8 interviewees

    The task

    • Apply for a loan
    • Authenticate through Singpass
    • Accept digital loan offer

    Results

    Overall, feedback was positive and there were no blockers. Users rated 4/5 for ease of use. However, there were 2 items we had to relooked into.

    Objectives:
    • Will user understand concept of "apply all in a go"
    • Are there sufficient info on landing page for for users to apply loan directly?
    • Will indicating product name help user identify what they want?

    Another discovery was realising users have difficulties uploading documents.

    UAT

    Maintaining design quality

    Challenge

    • Spending hours going back & forth on changes; UAT was 80% of workload
    • Tech team require us to create a Jira post for every bug, even if its simple content changes

    Solution

    Weekly meeting with tech to go through changes on the spot; 20% of workload

    Biggest takeaway

    Design for scaleability

    Since this project started during the covid period, information on the loan products we were working on changes frequently. We had to make sure the flow and design were scalable and flexible to the changes.
