Discovery & Define

Convincing stakeholders reskin will not solve the problems

Situation/constraints

Initially, the business team request was to reskin the loan application form. There was no clear direction & limited understanding about the pain points for our users (SMEs) as we had no researcher & limited resources on this project.​​​​​​​

Tasks

In order to convince stakeholders that reskin will not solve the problems, we did customer research through consolidated findings from​​​​​

Qualitative data:
  • User interviews recordings conducted in the past 
  • Interviews with customer-facing staff

    • Quantitative data:
  • Google analytics 
  • Customer care service calls

    • Results

    Consolidated findings:​​​​​​​

    After sharing our findings with the business team, they were convinced that a reskin will not solve the the real problem. The business team was on board to spend more time for discovery and to come up with a solution that solves the real problem.

    Design

    1. Data driven solutions

    Problem: Difficulties comparing loan products

    The 'before' page shown below was one of the most visited loan pages. Unfortunately visitor's feedback was "It is so difficult to understand the features of the loans DBS has. This page doesnt tell me anything."

    Additionally, there's a 30% dropoff rate from this page.

    Solution

  • Key details of loan products are shown above the fold to allow easy comparison
  • Labels to indicate government assisted loans

    • Result

    20% increment in applicants who started an application

    Problem: Applicants do not know what documents are required for application

    "I don't have my documents right now"

  • 40% dropped off on document page
  • 40-50% of applicants select upload documents later, increases turn around time
  • Some applicants don't have financial statements

    • Solution

  • NOA and NRIC to be auto-pulled from Singpass
  • Provide financial statements template to download

    • Result

  • 50% reduction in dropped off on document page
  • 33% selected upload documents later

    • 2. Managing business needs with design

    Business need

    Business insisted on displaying loan products on the website like items on catalog.

    1 product = 1 detail page & application form

    With many similar loan offerings, applicants cannot differentiate between similar loans. 40% applied wrong loan, increasing TAT by 30mins for each reassignment.

    Solution

    We shared our product research that compares each loan's features to convince business some products features are too similar.

    Then, suggested combining loans with similar purpose on UI level but retain business needs of keeping individual products.

    Result

    Collaborated with tech to design a flow where backend system helps to assign products based on eligibility checks: 0% of applicants applied for wrong products.

    3. Streamlining customer journey

    Problem

    All approved loans required applicants and guarantors to meet relationship managers physically to verify and accept loans.

    Solution

    Applicants & guarantors are now able to:
  • Verify identity with Singpass or manually by uploading IC & NOA
  • Accept loan offer online 

    • Result

  • 80% of applicants verified through Singpass
  • Atleast 50% of applicants qualified to accept loans online 

    • User validation

    Online user interviews with 8 interviewees

    The task

  • Apply for a loan
  • Authenticate through Singpass
  • Accept digital loan offer

    • Results

    Overall, feedback was positive and there were no blockers. Users rated 4/5 for ease of use. However, there were 2 items we had to relooked into.

    Objectives:
  • Will user understand concept of "apply all in a go"
  • Are there sufficient info on landing page for for users to apply loan directly?
  • Will indicating product name help user identify what they want?

    • Another discovery was realising users have difficulties uploading documents.

    Maintaining design quality

    Challenge

  • Spending hours going back & forth on changes; UAT was 80% of workload
  • Tech team require us to create a Jira post for every bug, even if its simple content changes

    • Solution

    Weekly meeting with tech to go through changes on the spot; 20% of workload

    Biggest takeaway

    Design for scaleability

    Since this project started during the covid period, information on the loan products we were working on changes frequently. We had to make sure the flow and design were scalable and flexible to the changes.
